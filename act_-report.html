INSIGHTS¶

The following insights were derived from the analysis:

  • Some dogs got 0/10 rating
  • Most of tweets have only one image.
  • All numeric variables have right skewed distribution, that means that the least values have occurred the most.
  • We have a good percentage of high prediction cases (> 0.8).
  • Most of dog ratings fall between 0 and 200, and we have extreem values reached >17500.
  • The only relationship we can mention here, is the strong positive correlation between the retweets count & favorite count. and that makes sense. The more images the tweet has, the more likely to get retweets & favorites.
  • Twitter for iPhone is the most used application for Twitter users of WeRateDogs.
  • Almost 75% of the results are True.
  • Multi_stages dogs have very little percentages.
  • Pupper & Doggo stages have more than 80% of the dogs.
  • Most of tweets had retweets less than 10,000 and favorites up to 32,000.
  • We have a few tweets that got much more favorites & retweets.
  • Alwayse the favorites count is bigger than the retweets count.
  • There is a slight increase in favorites & retweets overtime.
  • The biggest retweet count was about 75,000 and that was in June 2016.
  • The highest mean ratings are not associated to dogs at all.
  • I thought that number of images would result more confident results, but that seems not to be true.
  • Most of the high confidence results were based on 1 image.
